Transparency, emotions and many things.

My name is Alex, and I am the founder and CEO of the company commonly known as Moolah. I am a reclusive tech-orientated person by nature, I am passionate – and I have not been trained to speak in the manipulative ways that a lot of CEO’s do. I speak my mind, no matter what it is. Some people would warn me off from making this blog post, but I believe that our level of human interaction with the community, our investors and our customers… is one of the reasons that we’re so successful at this stage. I will continue to speak my mind, I’ll just learn to phrase things better.
 
As evidenced by some of my posts yesterday on the sub-reddit, I am only human, and sometimes I overreact. When an extremely prominent member of the community is demanding proof of my personal property records and a copy of my identification, on top of the information we have provided (we have gone above and beyond our legal obligations), I felt that a line had been crossed. I spoke with a number of people yesterday, including a lengthy video call with Jackson Palmer and Landon Merrill. Thankfully, I have to say that my (and Landon’s) interaction with Jackson was proven to be nothing but fruitful, and it is easy to tell that he is only somebody that cares greatly about the community that has arisen as a result of his actions. On top of Jackson, I would like to give a shout-out to Josh Mohland (the creator of dogetipbot) – he is just as hotheaded as myself, and we seem to get along fairly well now ;-).
 
It is great to see in the aftermath of this that the Dogecoin Foundation will finally have a democratic structure brought to it, and maybe the new foundation is something that our Moofund initiative can work with. Only time will tell.
 
People seem to think that we have been dodging questions, when the opposite is true. We have repeatedly answered the questions asked of us, time and time again. We have been more transparent than most, and provided every tidbit of information we could think to do so. When people started demanding copies of identification for myself and my staff, and then began to call myself and my team liars and con artists… again, I felt that a line had been crossed. This sentiment was shared by the members of my team, other prominent community members, and what seems to be the majority of the general public. As I have said time and time again, we love each and every one of you.
 
Jackson yesterday asked if I would stick around in the community, and while I do feel that my original reaction was quite harsh – I am going to take a different approach to /r/dogecoin in the future. To business related questions, I will respond with the professional distance that is required. To anything that is not, I will respond as myself. To trolls, haters and people spreading FUD? I will simply cease to acknowledge their existence. While yesterday I was responsible for a part of the drama, I stand by my statement that we were pushed in to it. With the way that certain highly prominent community members were acting, I was not going to sit back and let this happen. The follow-up conversation that happened as a result of it, only cemented the opinion of myself and Landon in this regard.
 
So, I do apologise for any drama that I caused as a result of my post, but I do not apologise for my reasoning behind it. I am tired of people claiming we are not transparent, despite being more so than most. I am tired of people claiming that we dodge questions, despite answering them each and every time. I am tired of people making public claims, without once attempting to reach out to us for any information beforehand. I could go on and on, but you get the general idea.
 
Look at what our company has managed to do in a short space of time. We have a working and proven consumer platform. A solid merchant platform with support for a wide range of coins, an in-store payment terminal, and full conversion to FIAT. A high-speed and easy to use exchange with both crypto/crypto and crypto/fiat support, with a fully fledged mobile trading application. We have had delays with case fabrication (which have now been resolved, which is about time) in regards to ATM units, but the devices themselves are solid. We have big plans for the rest of the year, and are only going to improve and expand.
 
Our system works. It is effective. It is secure. On top of the regular security audits that we perform, we have done public bug bounty programs and are constantly looking to improve our platform “just a little bit more”. We have 24/7 customer support and if you have an issue, you can email in and usually get a response within minutes.
