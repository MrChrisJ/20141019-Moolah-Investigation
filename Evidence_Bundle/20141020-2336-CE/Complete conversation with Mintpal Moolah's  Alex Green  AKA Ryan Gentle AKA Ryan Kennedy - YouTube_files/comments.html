Hey Ben, don’t believe the garbage that Ryan… err, Alex, says about defamation in the United States. In England, it’s easy to sue people for defamation (Scientologists do it all the time there). In the U.S., it’s far more difficult. Put briefly, for you to be convicted of libel or defamation, the plaintiff must prove in a court of law that ALL of the following occurred:

1. You made a false statement and claimed it was a fact.
2. You published the false statement in public view.
3. You neglected to perform research to determine if the statement could be false.
4. The false statement caused damage to the defendant’s reputation.
5. You knew that the false statement was malicious.

Numbers 4 and 5 are the trickiest ones to prove. Worse yet (for the plaintiff, not you), if he is a public figure – which he is – the plaintiff’s case becomes much harder to prove. (What if your research was wrong because someone else lied? Did you ever say this was a fact or was it merely an accusation?) Your lawyer would have a field day with this.
